Skating

Glide, spin, or roll — balance is non-negotiable.

About the Game

Skating comes in many forms: ice skating, roller skating, inline skating, and figure skating. It combines athletic ability with rhythm, style, and often a high tolerance for falling. Depending on the type, skating can be recreational, artistic, or fiercely competitive.

Basic Rules

  • Competitions can be timed races, judged performances, or team events.
  • Safety gear like helmets and pads are essential in roller/inline formats.
  • Scoring in artistic skating is based on technique, style, and execution.

Skills Needed

  • Balance and coordination
  • Strength and flexibility
  • Agility and control at speed
Quick Info

Players: Individual or pairs/teams

Equipment: Skates (ice, roller, or inline), protective gear

Field Size: Rinks, arenas, or open tracks
